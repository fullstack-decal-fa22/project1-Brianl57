About Me

Hi there! My name is Brian Le and I am a freshman majoring in EECS at UC Berkeley. I became interested in computer programming my senior year of highschool and started learning code the summer before coming to Berkeley. I took a course on Javascript taught by Gordon Zhu. Although I never finished the course, it became the spark to the beginning of my computer programming journey.

Currently, I am studying how to build computer programs, web development, and designing information devices. Other areas of computer science that I would like to explore is ML, AI, software development, computer data management, and game development.

I come from LA and love going on adventures and new experiences. One thing that I absoulutely missed from SoCal are the beaches.

I am a huge fan of boba, so one of my favorite things to do back in LA was trying new boba places. My favorite - four seasons latte (used to be Jasmie MT though) at Sunright.

I also love music. I listen to a lot of rock, indie-pop, and jazz. I play guitar and used to play saxophone back in highschool. Interesting fact, I was the drum major of my high school marching band my senior year (left picture).
